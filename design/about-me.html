My Journey

I am a Computer Science graduate with a strong interest in building products that solve real user problems. My journey into product management started through hands-on project work and later evolved through my experience as a Product Analyst, where I worked closely with product managers and engineers on requirement analysis, research, and product documentation.

I completed my Bachelor’s in Computer Science and Engineering with a CGPA of 3.93, building a strong foundation in software engineering, system design, data, and problem solving. Alongside academics, I actively worked on product-oriented projects and research, which helped me understand how technology, users, and business goals come together in real-world systems.

What draws me to product management is the opportunity to bridge user needs and execution. I enjoy breaking down ambiguous problems, understanding user behavior, and translating insights into structured workflows, features, and product decisions. My experience across projects, research, and industry exposure has strengthened my ability to think both analytically and from a user-first perspective.

I am particularly interested in solving problems related to productivity, student experiences, and multi-user systems where coordination, workflows, and usability play a critical role. I enjoy working on products that simplify complex processes and make everyday tasks more efficient and accessible.

Currently, I am seeking opportunities in Product Management where I can contribute to building meaningful products, learn from experienced teams, and grow into a product leader over time.
