Personal Introduction

I build with product thinking, not just code.

I am a Computer Science graduate with a strong interest in building products that solve real user problems. My path into product management started through hands-on project work and later became more focused through my experience as a Product Analyst, where I worked closely with product managers and engineers on requirement analysis, research, and product documentation.

What excites me most is turning unclear problems into meaningful product decisions, practical workflows, and user-centered experiences.

My academic journey gave me a strong base in software engineering, systems, data, and structured problem solving. Alongside that, working on projects and research helped me understand how technology, user behavior, and business goals come together in real-world products.

I am especially interested in the space between user needs and execution — understanding how people interact with systems, identifying where friction exists, and shaping features that make products simpler, clearer, and more useful.

The kinds of problems I naturally enjoy working on are related to productivity, student experiences, and multi-user systems, where coordination, workflows, and usability have a direct impact on how well the product serves people.
