I am a Computer Science graduate with a strong interest in building products that solve real user problems. My journey into product management started through hands-on project work and later evolved through my experience as a Product Analyst, where I worked closely with product managers and engineers on requirement analysis, research, and product documentation.

My academic background gave me a solid foundation in software engineering, systems, data, and structured problem solving. Alongside academics, I worked on product-oriented projects and research that helped me understand how technology, user needs, and business goals come together in real-world systems.

What draws me most to product management is the opportunity to bridge user needs and execution. I enjoy breaking down ambiguous problems, understanding user behavior, and translating insights into workflows, features, and decisions that make products more useful, scalable, and practical.
