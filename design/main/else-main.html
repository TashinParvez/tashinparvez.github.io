Background

I'm a Computer Science graduate with a genuine interest in building products that solve real user problems. My path into product management started through hands-on project work and deepened through my role as a Product Analyst, where I worked closely with PMs and engineers on requirement analysis, research, and product documentation.

I graduated with a CGPA of 3.93, building a solid foundation in software engineering, system design, and data — while also staying deeply active in competitive programming, which sharpened how I break down and approach complex problems.

What drives me

What draws me to product management is the chance to bridge user needs with execution — breaking down ambiguous problems, understanding behaviour, and translating insights into structured features and product decisions.

I'm especially interested in productivity tools, student experiences, and multi-user systems where coordination, workflows, and usability play a critical role. I enjoy working on products that simplify complex processes and make everyday tasks more accessible.

Beyond the work

I genuinely enjoy helping others grow. I regularly share study materials, problem-solving approaches, and learning resources with peers — a habit that has sharpened how I communicate complex ideas and naturally developed a leadership mindset. I think in systems, and I care about making things better for the people using them.
